Intersymbol Interference (ISI)

ISI occurs when the "tail" of one pulse overlaps with adjacent symbol periods, corrupting detection. Sources: band-limited channels, multipath propagation, imperfect filtering.

The received signal at sampling instant $t = kT_b$:

$$r(kT_b) = a_k\,p(0) + \underbrace{\sum_{n\neq k}a_n\,p((k-n)T_b)}_{\text{ISI}} + n(kT_b)$$

For zero ISI, we need the Nyquist criterion: $p(nT_b) = 0$ for all $n \neq 0$.

Nyquist ISI Criterion

A pulse $p(t)$ satisfies the Nyquist criterion for zero ISI if and only if its spectrum satisfies:

$$\sum_k P\!\left(f - \frac{k}{T_b}\right) = T_b \quad \text{for all } f$$

The simplest solution is the ideal sinc pulse ($P(f) = T_b\,\text{rect}(fT_b)$), but it decays as $1/|t|$ — impractical. The raised cosine family provides faster decay.

Raised Cosine (RC) Filter

The frequency response introduces a smooth rolloff controlled by the roll-off factor $\alpha \in [0,1]$:

$$H_{RC}(f) = \begin{cases}T_b & |f| \leq \frac{1-\alpha}{2T_b}\\ \frac{T_b}{2}\left[1+\cos\!\left(\frac{\pi T_b}{\alpha}\left(|f|-\frac{1-\alpha}{2T_b}\right)\right)\right] & \frac{1-\alpha}{2T_b}<|f|\leq\frac{1+\alpha}{2T_b}\\0&\text{otherwise}\end{cases}$$

The corresponding impulse response:

$$p(t) = \text{sinc}\!\left(\frac{t}{T_b}\right)\frac{\cos(\pi\alpha t/T_b)}{1-(2\alpha t/T_b)^2}$$

Properties:

  • $\alpha = 0$: Minimum bandwidth $1/(2T_b)$, slowest decay ($\sim 1/|t|$)
  • $\alpha = 1$: Maximum bandwidth $1/T_b$, fastest decay ($\sim 1/|t|^3$) — most practical
  • Bandwidth: $B = (1+\alpha)/(2T_b)$
$$B_{RC} = \frac{1+\alpha}{2T_b} = \frac{R_s(1+\alpha)}{2}$$

Root Raised Cosine (RRC)

In practice, the raised cosine is split between transmitter and receiver: $H_{TX}(f) = H_{RX}(f) = \sqrt{H_{RC}(f)}$.

$$H_{RRC}(f) = \sqrt{H_{RC}(f)}$$

This achieves:

  • Zero ISI at the cascade output: $H_{TX}(f)\cdot H_{RX}(f) = H_{RC}(f)$
  • Matched filtering at the receiver (optimal for AWGN)

Matched Filter

The matched filter maximizes the output SNR at the sampling instant. For a transmitted pulse $s(t)$:

$$h_{MF}(t) = s(T_b - t) \qquad H_{MF}(f) = S^*(f)\,e^{-j2\pi f T_b}$$

Maximum output SNR:

$$\text{SNR}_{max} = \frac{2E_b}{N_0}$$

where $E_b$ is the energy per bit and $N_0/2$ is the two-sided noise PSD. This is the best achievable SNR — independent of pulse shape.

Derivation: Matched filter optimality

The output at $t = T_b$:

$$y(T_b) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}S(f)H(f)\,e^{j2\pi fT_b}df$$

Noise power at output:

$$\sigma_n^2 = \frac{N_0}{2}\int|H(f)|^2 df$$

By the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality:

$$\text{SNR} = \frac{|y(T_b)|^2}{\sigma_n^2} \leq \frac{2}{N_0}\int|S(f)|^2 df = \frac{2E_s}{N_0}$$

Equality holds when $H(f) = kS^*(f)e^{-j2\pi fT_b}$, i.e., the matched filter.
