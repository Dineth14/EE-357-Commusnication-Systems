Overview

Digital modulation maps binary data onto carrier signal parameters (amplitude, frequency, or phase). The choice of scheme involves trade-offs between bandwidth efficiency (bits/s/Hz), power efficiency (BER at given $E_b/N_0$), and implementation complexity.

Amplitude Shift Keying (ASK)

Binary ASK (OOK — On-Off Keying) switches the carrier on/off:

$$s(t) = \begin{cases}A_c\cos(2\pi f_c t) & \text{bit '1'}\\0 & \text{bit '0'}\end{cases}$$

Coherent detection BER: $P_e = Q\!\left(\sqrt{E_b/N_0}\right)$. Simple but poor noise performance.

Frequency Shift Keying (FSK)

Binary FSK uses two distinct frequencies for 0 and 1:

$$s(t) = A_c\cos(2\pi f_i t), \quad f_i = \begin{cases}f_1 & \text{bit '1'}\\f_2 & \text{bit '0'}\end{cases}$$
$$P_e^{coh} = Q\!\left(\sqrt{E_b/N_0}\right), \qquad P_e^{ncoh} = \frac{1}{2}e^{-E_b/(2N_0)}$$

Binary Phase Shift Keying (BPSK)

BPSK encodes data in the carrier phase (0° or 180°):

$$s(t) = A_c\cos(2\pi f_c t + \phi), \quad \phi = \begin{cases}0 & \text{bit '1'}\\\pi & \text{bit '0'}\end{cases}$$

Equivalently: $s(t) = (\pm 1)\cdot A_c\cos(2\pi f_c t)$. BER:

$$P_e = Q\!\left(\sqrt{\frac{2E_b}{N_0}}\right)$$

BPSK is the most power-efficient binary scheme — 3 dB better than coherent FSK/ASK.

Differential PSK (DPSK)

Avoids the need for a coherent reference by encoding data in phase changes:

$$\phi_k = \phi_{k-1} + \Delta\phi, \quad \Delta\phi = \begin{cases}0 & \text{bit '1'}\\\pi & \text{bit '0'}\end{cases}$$
$$P_e = \frac{1}{2}e^{-E_b/N_0}$$

About 1 dB worse than coherent BPSK, but much simpler receiver (no carrier recovery needed).

Quadrature PSK (QPSK)

QPSK transmits 2 bits per symbol using four phase states:

$$s(t) = A_c\cos(2\pi f_c t + \theta_k), \quad \theta_k \in \{45°, 135°, 225°, 315°\}$$

Equivalently, using I-Q representation:

$$s(t) = I_k\cos(2\pi f_c t) - Q_k\sin(2\pi f_c t)$$

where $I_k, Q_k \in \{+1, -1\}$. QPSK has the same BER per bit as BPSK but twice the spectral efficiency (2 bits/s/Hz vs 1 bit/s/Hz).

M-ary QAM

QAM modulates both amplitude and phase — combining ASK and PSK:

$$s(t) = A_I\cos(2\pi f_c t) - A_Q\sin(2\pi f_c t)$$

For $M$-QAM: $\log_2 M$ bits per symbol. Common values: 16-QAM (4 bits), 64-QAM (6 bits), 256-QAM (8 bits).

$$P_e \approx 4\left(1-\frac{1}{\sqrt{M}}\right)Q\!\left(\sqrt{\frac{3E_b\log_2 M}{(M-1)N_0}}\right) \quad \text{(approx. for square QAM)}$$
