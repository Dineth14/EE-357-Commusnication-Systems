# Simulation Output Plots

This directory contains the graphical outputs generated by the simulation scripts in this repository.

## Files

### [natural_sampling_plots.png](natural_sampling_plots.png)
- **Description**: Visualizes the process of natural sampling and demodulation.
- **Contents**:
  - **Time Domain**: Original signal, Switching Signal (Pulse Train), Sampled Signal, Demodulated Signal.
  - **Frequency Domain**: Original Spectrum, Sampled Spectrum (showing harmonics), Demodulated Spectrum (showing baseband recovery).
- **Source Script**: [Natural_sampling.py](../Sampling/Natural_sampling.py)
- **Parameters**: $B=5$ Hz, $f_s=20$ Hz, $d=1/3$.

### [Square_Law_Modulation_Output.png](Square_Law_Modulation_Output.png)
- **Description**: Visualizes Amplitude Modulation using Square Law Modulation.
- **Contents**:
  - Unmodulated Carrier and Message Signals.
  - Modulated Signal in Time Domain.
  - Frequency Spectrum showing Carrier and Sidebands.
- **Source Script**: [Square_Law_Modulation.py](../Modulation/Square_Law_Modulation.py)

### [Flat_Top_Sampling_Output.png](Flat_Top_Sampling_Output.png)
- **Description**: Visualizes Flat Top Sampling (Sample and Hold) and the Aperture Effect.
- **Contents**:
  - **Time Domain**: Original vs held pulses.
  - **Demodulation**: Recovered signal using LPF.
  - **Frequency Domain**: Spectrum showing the "sinc" roll-off (Aperture Effect) affecting the harmonics.
- **Source Script**: [Flat_Top_Sampling.py](../Sampling/Flat_Top_Sampling.py)

### [PWM_Output.png](PWM_Output.png)
- **Description**: Visualizes Pulse Width Modulation (Trailing-Edge).
- **Contents**:
  - **Modulation**: Pulses with variable width $\tau_n = \tau_0 + k_w m(nT_s)$.
  - **Demodulation**: Recovered signal via Low Pass Filtering.
- **Source Script**: [PWM.py](../Modulation/PWM.py)

### [PPM_Output.png](PPM_Output.png)
- **Description**: Visualizes Pulse Position Modulation.
- **Contents**:
  - **Modulation**: Fixed-width pulses shifted from clock ticks by $k_p m(nT_s)$.
  - **Conversion**: Intermediate conversion to PWM for recovery.
  - **Demodulation**: Signal recovered from converted PWM.
- **Source Script**: [PPM.py](../Modulation/PPM.py)

### [SSB_Output.png](SSB_Output.png)
- **Description**: Visualizes Single Sideband (SSB) Modulation via Hilbert Transform.
- **Contents**:
  - **Time Domain**: Comparison of USSB/LSSB vs DSB-SC. USSB and LSSB of a tone are constant envelope tones at $\omega_c+\omega_m$ and $\omega_c-\omega_m$.
  - **Frequency Domain**: Spectrum showing the selection of a single sideband (Upper or Lower) while rejecting the other.
- **Source Script**: [SSB_Modulation.py](../Modulation/SSB_Modulation.py)

### [PCM_Output.png](PCM_Output.png)
- **Description**: Visualizes Pulse Code Modulation (Sampling & Quantization).
- **Contents**:
  - **Time Domain**: Analog signal vs 3-bit quantized "staircase" signal.
  - **Bit Encoding**: Demonstrates conversion of levels to Sample Codes.
  - **Error Analysis**: Quantization noise confined within $\pm \Delta/2$.
  - **SNR Verification**: Compares simulated SNR vs Theoretical ($1.76 + 6n$ dB) for 3-bit and 8-bit systems.
- **Source Script**: [PCM.py](../Sampling/PCM.py)

### [SSB_Analysis_Horns.png](SSB_Analysis_Horns.png)
- **Description**: Visualizes the "Horn Effect" in SSB modulation of square waves.
- **Contents**: 
  - Shows the infinite peaks in the Hilbert Transform of an ideal square wave.
  - Demonstrates how pre-filtering (LPF) smooths the signal and removes these spikes.

### [SSB_Analysis_Weaver.png](SSB_Analysis_Weaver.png)
- **Description**: Spectrum output of Weaver's Modulator simulation.
- **Contents**: Shows the generated Single Sideband signal using the "Third Method" (two-stage mixing) without wideband phase shifters.
- **Source Script**: [SSB_Analysis.py](../Modulation/SSB_Analysis.py)

### [VSB_Filter_Response.png](VSB_Filter_Response.png)
- **Description**: Visualizes the VSB Shaping Filter and its Symmetry Condition.
- **Contents**: 
  - Trace of $H(f)$ showing the linear roll-off region.
  - Verification that $H(f-f_c) + H(f+f_c) = 1$ at symmetric points, ensuring distortion-free recovery.

### [Mixing_Analysis.png](Mixing_Analysis.png)
- **Description**: Spectrum output of a Frequency Translation (Mixing) operation.
- **Contents**:
  - Shows the generation of Sum and Difference frequencies from an Input and LO.
  - Demonstrates the principle behind Up-Conversion and Down-Conversion.
- **Source Script**: [VSB_Mixing_Analysis.py](../Modulation/VSB_Mixing_Analysis.py)

### [Lab2_Output.png](../labs/Lab2_Output.png)
- **Description**: Results of Lab 2 (AM Synchronous Demodulation).
- **Location**: Saved in `labs/` folder.
- **Contents**:
    - Waveforms of Message and Carrier.
    - AM Signal with $\mu=0.8$.
    - Comparison of Original vs Demodulated signal (recovered via product detector + LPF).
- **Source Script**: [Lab2_AM_Demod.py](../labs/Lab2_AM_Demod.py)
